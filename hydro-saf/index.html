HYDRO-SAF

Performance and material compatibility of Sustainable Aviation Fuels

HYDRO-SAF investigates the impact of hydrotreated Sustainable Aviation Fuel blends on engine performance, emissions and elastomeric material compatibility under realistic operating conditions.

Core research areas

Main scientific objectives addressed in the HYDRO-SAF subproject.

Fuel characterization

Experimental determination of physical and chemical properties of fossil kerosene (JP8) and SAF blends to support performance and emissions analysis.

Material compatibility

Assessment of degradation, durability and mechanical behaviour of elastomeric materials exposed to SAF under controlled conditions.

Engine performance & emissions

Evaluation of thrust, fuel consumption and pollutant emissions in micro-jet and turbojet engines using SAF blends.

Experimental methodology from laboratory to real engines

The project combines controlled laboratory experiments with micro-jet engines and validation at larger scale using turbojet systems. Tests are performed under representative operating conditions, including landing and take-off cycles, enabling a realistic assessment of Sustainable Aviation Fuel behaviour.

Scientific scope

Fuel blends analysis

Comparison between conventional JP8 fuel and different SAF blends to identify variations in combustion behaviour and emissions.

Elastomer degradation

Immersion and mechanical testing to evaluate material compatibility, including hardness, wear and structural integrity.

Emission characterization

Measurement of CO₂, CO, NOx, hydrocarbons and particle emissions under different engine loads.

Performance validation

Analysis of thrust, fuel consumption and efficiency to ensure safe operation without performance loss.
