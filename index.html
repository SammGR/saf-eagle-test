SAF-EAGLE: Technological and environmental viability of Sustainable Aviation Fuels

Coordinated research project focused on evaluating the effects of Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAF) on engine performance, material compatibility and ground-level emissions.

Main Objective: Assessment of the technical viability of Sustainable Aviation Fuels in micro-jet engines, turbojet engines and aircraft under different operating conditions.

SAF-EAGLE Project

Coordinated Structure

Three interconnected subprojects covering fuels, engines and emissions.

HYDRO-SAF

Experimental analysis of performance, emissions and compatibility of elastomeric materials using SAF blends in jet engines.

SAF-FALCON

Production, upgrading and characterization of new sustainable aviation fuels from alternative sources such as plastic waste.

METHOD-EAGLE

Development of advanced methodologies for comprehensive characterization of gaseous and particulate emissions in aviation.

SAF-EAGLE Project

Justification and Coordination

Integrated research strategy based on coordinated experimental work.

Need for coordination

The complexity of SAF evaluation requires collaboration between specialized research groups combining complementary expertise.

Common experimental framework

All subprojects share fuels, operating conditions and test campaigns to ensure comparability and consistency of results.

Multi-scale approach

Research progresses from laboratory-scale micro-jet engines to real turbojet engines and aircraft testing.

Dual-scale experimental strategy

Combination of micro-jet engine testing and full-scale validation in real aircraft engines.

Integrated analysis

Simultaneous study of fuel properties, engine performance and pollutant emissions.

Advanced methodologies

SAF-EAGLE Project

Objectives and Methodology

From laboratory to real aviation systems

The methodology integrates experimental campaigns, modelling and coordinated testing to ensure reliable evaluation of SAF under realistic conditions.

Fuel characterization

Analysis of physical-chemical properties and development of new SAF including pyrolysis-based fuels.

Experimental validation

Testing in micro-jet engines followed by validation in real engines and aircraft.

Emission analysis

Comprehensive measurement of gaseous and particulate emissions using advanced instrumentation.
